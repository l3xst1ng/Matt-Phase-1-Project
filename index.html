About Tardy Spector

Tardy Spector is a web-based application developed to streamline the process of monitoring student attendance in educational institutions. With a user-friendly interface and robust backend functionality, the app aims to simplify attendance tracking for both students and educators.

Key Features

  • Efficient Attendance Management

    The app automates the attendance recording process, eliminating the need for manual tracking and reducing administrative burden.

  • Real-time Updates

    Students can check in to record their attendance, and educators can instantly access up-to-date attendance records through the app.

  • Customizable Reporting

    The app provides comprehensive attendance reports, allowing educators to analyze attendance patterns and identify areas for improvement.

  • Secure Authentication

    Built-in authentication ensures secure access to the app, protecting student data and maintaining privacy.

Purpose

The primary goal of the Student Attendance Tracking App is to promote accountability and foster a culture of punctuality among students. By providing educators with accurate attendance data and empowering students to take ownership of their attendance records, the app supports academic success and enhances the overall learning experience.

Whether used in traditional classrooms, online courses, or hybrid learning environments, the Student Attendance Tracking App serves as a valuable tool for educators and students alike, promoting engagement, accountability, and academic excellence.

Attendance Policies and Rules

This section highlights the school's attendance policies, rules, and consequences for tardiness or absenteeism as outlined in the student contract. All students are expected to carefully review and understand these attendance expectations and the importance of personal accountability.

  1. Punctuality

    • Students are expected to arrive at school on time, ready to begin their day. The school gates open at 6:00 AM, and students should be in their classrooms by 9:00 AM.

    • Any student arriving after 9:05 AM will be marked as Late.

    • Any Student arriving after 9:40 AM will be marked Absent.

  2. Attendance Tracking

    • All students are required to check-in using the Tardy Spector app upon arrival at school. Failure to check-in will result in being marked as absent.

  3. Excused Absences

    • Absences due to illness, medical appointments, family emergencies, or religious observances will be considered excused if prior notice is given is provided to the school office within three days of the absence.

    • For extended absences (two or more consecutive days), a written note from is required.

  4. Unexcused Absences

    • Absences without a valid excuse or proper documentation will be marked as unexcused.

    • Three or more unexcused absences will result in disciplinary action.

  5. Early Dismissal

    • Students leaving school before the end of the scheduled day must inform the school administration.

    • Early dismissals for reasons other than medical or emergencies will be marked as unexcused, unless prior approval is obtained from the school administration.

  6. Truancy

    • Truancy, defined as an unexcused absence from school without school and parental/sponsor/guardian knowledge or consent, is strictly prohibited.

    • Truancy will result in disciplinary action, including, suspension from the program, or escalation to the appropriate authorities. The school will not be responsible for any action that befalls truants.

  7. Attendance Notifications

    • Students and Parents/guardians will receive automated notifications via email, or SMS for and tardiness, early dismissal, or unexcused absence.

    • Consistent tardiness or absenteeism will result in action from school administrators to address the issue.

  8. Attendance Records

    • Attendance records are maintained by the school and are part of a student's permanent record.

    • Accurate attendance data is essential for compliance with school policies and regulations and will impact student graduation and eligibility to proceed to scheduled programs.
